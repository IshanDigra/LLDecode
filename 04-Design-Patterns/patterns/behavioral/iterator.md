# Iterator Design Pattern

## Definition
The Iterator pattern provides a way to access elements of a collection sequentially without exposing its underlying representation.

## Concept
- The collection provides an iterator object.
- The iterator exposes methods to traverse the collection (e.g., next(), hasNext()).

## Analogy
A tour guide leads visitors through a museum, one exhibit at a time, without the visitors needing to know the layout.

## Benefits
- Simplifies collection traversal.
- Decouples collection structure from traversal logic.
